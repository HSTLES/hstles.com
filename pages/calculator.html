Common Questions

How does the Engagement Fee credit work?

Your Engagement Fee isn't a sunk cost - it becomes a credit toward your monthly lease! Once your solution goes live, the engagement fee is applied as payment for your first months of platform access. For example, if your engagement fee is $4,000 and your monthly lease is $2,000, your first 2 months are already paid. You're not paying twice for the same thing.

Why is HSTLES cheaper than a freelancer?

We've built the platform once and reuse it across clients. You're not paying for us to build authentication, hosting infrastructure, or security from scratch every time. You're paying for your specific business logic on top of a proven foundation.

What if my needs are too complex for low-code?

That's exactly our sweet spot. Low-code works great for simple apps, but hits walls with complex business logic, custom integrations, or specific compliance requirements. HSTLES gives you real custom development without the traditional cost.

What happens if I want to leave HSTLES?

We provide a full export of your data in standard formats. Your business logic is documented in the Scope Pack. You're never locked in - but we're confident you won't want to leave once you experience the service.

Why compare Total Cost of Ownership?

Most quotes only show the build cost. But software costs don't end at launch. You need to consider: hosting (infrastructure, scaling, backups), maintenance (security patches, bug fixes, monitoring), support (help desk, incident response), and enhancements (new features as your business grows). Our calculator shows you the complete picture over 3 years - the real cost of ownership.

What's included in the HSTLES lease?

Everything except new features: hosting on secure Australian infrastructure, backups and disaster recovery, monitoring and performance management, security patches and platform updates, SLA-backed support during your tier's hours, and defect fixes for anything that breaks. You get a working service, not just code to maintain.
