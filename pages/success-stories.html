Equipment Hire

Regional Equipment Hire

Replacing spreadsheet chaos with a booking system that actually works

The Problem

A family-owned equipment hire business in regional NSW had grown to manage over 200 pieces of equipment across two depots. Their system was a mess:

  • Shared Excel spreadsheet on Dropbox (frequently corrupted)
  • Wall calendars at each depot (often out of sync)
  • Sticky notes for maintenance reminders
  • Invoices generated manually in Word

Double-bookings were common. Invoices were forgotten. The owner estimated $2,000-3,000/month lost to missed charges and inefficiency.

The Solution

We built a focused equipment management system:

  • Equipment registry — Service history, location, availability
  • Booking calendar — Real-time view across both depots
  • Pre-hire checklists — Digital sign-off before equipment leaves
  • Automated invoicing — Synced to MYOB
  • Maintenance alerts — Service reminders by hours or calendar

The Results

Zero double-bookings8 months since go-live
~$2,500/month recoveredPreviously missed hire charges
Same-day invoicingDown from "whenever someone remembered"
Maintenance complianceNo overdue equipment goes out
"We looked at off-the-shelf hire software but nothing fit how we actually work. HSTLES built exactly what we needed — no more, no less. Pays for itself every month."
— Owner/Director

Manufacturing

Steel Fabrication Workshop

From scattered orders and whiteboard scheduling to unified order-to-delivery

The Problem

A custom steel fabrication workshop making structural steel, gates, and specialty metalwork for builders, engineers, and direct customers. Orders came from everywhere:

  • Phone calls scribbled on notepads that got lost
  • Emails sitting unread in shared inboxes
  • Walk-in customers expecting instant quotes
  • Builder portal orders that needed manual re-entry

Jobs were tracked on whiteboards, quotes lived in desktop folders, and delivery scheduling was a daily argument about which truck went where. They'd regularly double-book vehicles or discover jobs weren't ready when drivers arrived.

The Solution

We built a unified order-to-delivery system bringing everything into one place:

  • Central order intake — Phone, email, walk-in, and portal orders all enter the same queue
  • Quoting module — Material calculators, saved templates, quick turnaround
  • Workshop job board — What's in progress, what's next, what's waiting on materials
  • Delivery scheduling — Linked to job completion status, no more guessing
  • Customer notifications — Quote ready, job started, out for delivery

The Results

Quoting time slashedFrom 2 hours average down to 20 minutes
Delivery failures near zeroDown from 3-4 wasted trips per week
No more lost ordersEverything captured, nothing forgotten
Workshop visibilityReal-time job status replaced daily standups
"We used to lose jobs because quotes sat in someone's inbox for days. Now everything flows through the system — order comes in, quote goes out, job gets scheduled, truck gets loaded. Simple."
— Workshop Manager
  • 20 weeks
  • Custom SaaS
  • S2 SLA

The Problem

A regional allied health practice with 12 practitioners had been running their entire operation on a Microsoft Access database built in 2009. Patient records, scheduling, NDIS claims, and invoicing all managed through one ageing Windows server.

The risks were mounting:

  • Original developer retired — nobody understood the VBA code
  • Windows Server 2012 approaching end of extended support

The Solution

We rebuilt their core workflows onto the HSTLES platform:

  • Patient management — Records, contacts, referral tracking
  • Appointment scheduling — Calendar with practitioner availability
  • NDIS claim preparation — Formatted for bulk portal upload
  • Invoicing — Integrated with Xero

Full data migration with validation. Two half-day training sessions. Old server decommissioned.
